About

I am an undergraduate student at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University, pursuing a BSc (Hons) in Land Surveying and Geo-Informatics.

My academic interests lie at the intersection of geospatial data science, system dynamics, and environmental modelling. I am particularly drawn to understanding how spatial patterns, feedback structures, and long-term system behaviour shape urban and environmental outcomes.

I have gained exposure to SAR/InSAR-based deformation monitoring, GIS spatial analysis, and system-level modelling through research experiences at MIT, Tsinghua University, and PolyU. These opportunities have deepened my understanding of how remote sensing, spatial analytics, and systems thinking can be integrated to address complex sustainability challenges.

Beyond academics, I am committed to sustainability advocacy and interdisciplinary collaboration. I have participated in environmental forums, led student initiatives, and engaged in cross-cultural exchanges to promote sustainable development and innovative problem-solving.

I am eager to continue exploring the intersection of geospatial technology, system dynamics, and sustainability science, with the goal of contributing to evidence-based solutions for urban and environmental resilience.
